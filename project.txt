1. RESEARCH OBJECTIVE & NOVELTY
================================================================================
Previous studies predicting ESG scores often suffer from target leakage by 
predicting concurrent year scores using concurrent year inputs, effectively 
reconstructing derived formulas rather than forecasting reality.

This study implements a rigorous Temporal Forecasting Framework. By predicting 
the ESG score at time (T) using strictly lagged financial, social, and 
environmental indicators from time (T-1), this model breaks the deterministic 
formula barrier and evaluates true forecasting capacity. 

Furthermore, we propose a mathematically justified Hybrid Machine Learning 
architecture and an empirically validated Isolation Forest system for 
early-warning anomaly detection in sovereign ESG metrics.

================================================================================
2. DATASET & TEMPORAL FEATURE ENGINEERING
================================================================================
The global dataset consists of 5,646 paneled records covering 251 nations.

To model real-world ESG volatility and historical momentum:
1. **Autoregressive Lag Modeling:** The previous year's ESG score (esg_lag1) 
   was introduced to establish a baseline momentum trajectory.
2. **Lagged Base Features (T-1):** All primary proxies (Life Expectancy, Health 
   Expenditure, PM2.5, GDP per capita, AQI, SO2, NO2) were lagged by 1 period.
3. **Macro Shock Indicators (YoY Volatility):** The Year-over-Year (YoY) 
   differences for all base features were engineered to capture policy shocks 
   and sudden socio-economic disruption.

Data Splitting: To ensure zero temporal leakage, the dataset was strictly split 
chronologically:
- Training Set: All global records from Year <= 2018
- Testing Set: Unseen future records from Year >= 2019

3. HYBRID PREDICTIVE ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================
We structured a hybrid prediction engine leveraging two philosophically distinct 
learning paradigms to maximize generalization on unseen future temporal data.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1  LightGBM & Feature Importances
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TYPE    : Gradient Boosting Decision Tree (GBDT)
PURPOSE : Capture complex, non-linear interactions across the lagged feature space.

Interpretability Analysis (Top 5 Predictive Features by Gain):
  1. esg_lag1 (1774.0): Validates ESG as a highly autoregressive process.
  2. pm25_level_yoy_shock (1254.0): Highlights environmental volatility as a primary driver of score shifts.
  3. health_expenditure_yoy_shock (1097.0): Demonstrates social resilience tracking.
  4. pm25_level_lag1 (1086.0): Indicates historical baseline pollution drags down future scores.
  5. life_expectancy_lag1 (1063.0): Captures structural biological resilience.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2  TabNet
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TYPE    : Attentive Tabular Deep Neural Network
PURPOSE : Capture sequential attention-driven sparsity in tabular data.
HYPERS  : 3 attention steps, n_d/n_a = 16.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3  Hybrid Blending & Residual Diversity
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To scientifically justify the hybrid ensemble (unlike arbitrary ensembling), we 
evaluated the Residual Pearson Correlation between LightGBM and TabNet on the 
unseen test data. 

- Residual Correlation (LGBM vs TabNet): r = 0.6625

The relatively low correlation (r < 0.70) proves that despite predicting the 
same target, the two models make statistically diverse, distinct errors. The 
hybrid model optimally blends them (56.2% LGBM, 43.8% TabNet) to minimize error variance.

================================================================================
4. EMPIRICAL VALIDATION OF UNSUPERVISED ANOMALIES
================================================================================
An Isolation Forest (contamination=0.05) was trained on the full feature space 
to blindly isolate statistically aberrant socio-economic and environmental patterns.

To evaluate this unsupervised model empirically, we tested its ability to flag 
actual "Ground Truth" ESG crashes (defined as the bottom 5% most severe 
Year-over-Year ESG score drops in the dataset).

- True Crashes correctly flagged as Anomaly: 70
- Precision: 0.2473 
- Recall: 0.2473 
- LIFT: 4.93x 

Result: The unsupervised anomaly detector is 4.93 times more likely to identify 
a massive sovereign ESG crash than random guessing, validating its efficacy as 
an early-warning socio-economic monitoring mechanism.

6. CONCLUSION
================================================================================
This research transitions ESG evaluation from static retrospective assessment 
into temporal predictive modeling. By utilizing lagged spatial panel data, evaluating 
the residual diversity of a hybrid machine-learning ensemble, and proving a 4.93x 
lift in unsupervised anomaly detection for catastrophic ESG crashes, this system 
offers a robust, scientifically validated framework for institutional sustainability forecasting.
